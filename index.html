• A Shakespearean tragedy typically involves a protagonist of noble stature facing a tragic downfall due to a fatal flaw or external circumstances. These tragedies explore themes of fate, ambition, and morality, often resulting in the protagonist's death and a sense of catharsis for the audience, reflecting on the complexities of the human condition.
